We empower communities around the world
to monitor their environment.



Part of the City Scanner project, Flatburn is an open-source, 3D-printed environmental sensor.



Flatburn is powered using a solar panel and can be mounted on vehicles using magnetic pads.



The core module houses the electronics, antenna, and data storage board.



The sensing module houses heat, noise, and air quality sensors.



Build your own Flatburn and help your community monitor its environment.

Why the asterisk?



Flatburn*

When we were developing the first City Scanner, we were also reflecting about the importance of female leadership in science. While doing research on women in science at MIT for Mind the Gap, we came across the story of Kathryn A. Burns, the first woman to graduate from the MIT-WHOI Joint Program in Oceanography. Coincidentally, the designer of the device, Tom Benson, is from an industrial town in England called Blackburn. The first fully autonomous City Scanner, 3D-printed in black nylon, was named Blackburn, and ended up receiving a honorable mention at the Fast Company Innovation by Design Award. The following versions were named Whiteburn and Flatburn.
